# Company Tailoring

## Information To Use

Use company-specific evidence in this priority:

1. Job description and role requirements supplied by the user.
2. Company website or recruitment page supplied by the user.
3. User's own reason for interest.
4. Stable public knowledge only if already known; browse only when the user asks for current research.

Do not fabricate company facts. If the company information is missing, write a general version and list what to research.

## Tailoring Method

Connect three points:

```text
applicant experience -> company/role characteristic -> future contribution
```

Example:

```text
研究で培った仮説検証力 -> データを活用した事業改善に取り組む職種 -> 顧客課題を定量的に捉え、改善提案につなげる
```

## Industry Angles

Trading company:

- Cross-cultural coordination
- Business creation
- Stakeholder alignment
- Market and supply-chain perspective

Manufacturer:

- Product quality
- Technology and production
- Customer needs
- Long-term improvement

IT or software:

- User problem solving
- Data, product, engineering, or operation improvement
- Learning speed
- Team collaboration

Consulting:

- Problem structuring
- Logical communication
- Client impact
- Fast learning under ambiguity

Finance:

- Trust and precision
- Risk awareness
- Long-term customer relationship
- Numerical analysis

## Red Flags

Avoid saying:

- The company is a stepping stone.
- The applicant wants only training, visa stability, salary, or prestige.
- The company is attractive for vague global scale only.
- The applicant can contribute without showing a relevant basis.
